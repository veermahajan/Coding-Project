Chess Links

  • Chess.com is the world's biggest and most popular chess website. Here, you can play against others, learn by watching videos and puzzles, form clubs, discuss chess events and stay up-to-date with them, and much, much more! You can sign up for free.

  • My chess website is not the world's biggest and most popular chess website. Here, you can view videos and look at non-interactive puzzles.

  • 2700 Chess is a fantastic website on which you can stay up-to-date with current chess events and news. There are rating lists that are updated in real time which you can even embed in your own website! In addition, there are rating lists from history, detailed pages about chess players, and much, much, more!

  • Kasparov Chess is another great chess website. You can play games against others, solve puzzles with different themes and skill levels, and view many lessons. These lessons are from some of the world's strongest players, and range in theme and difficulty. Garry Kasparov, one of the world's best chess players of all time, and a former world champion, also has his own masterclass on the website. You can sign up for free and get access to lessons from some of the best players in the world.
